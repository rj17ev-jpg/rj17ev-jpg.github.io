## What this is

AVOIDANCE. I really hate how easy it is to avoid things. it's self-sabotage to the worst degree. It's running off completely in the wrong direction, hiding from the most fundamental & AUTHENTIC parts of yourself - hiding from your greatest desires, because the things you love and want the most are also the things that are the most capable of hurting you. They are the scary, exciting, challenging things that'll change your whole life for the better, because they'll teach you something even if you fail horribly. They'll be the things that actually make you *feel something*, things above and beyond the mundane and the day-to-day.

up to now avoidance has been a giant problem in my life. one might even say, it's the thing that has created the most distress, the most anxiety, the most negative consequences; what has been the reason for failure, again and again and again. 

I'd like to finally learn something from this. I don't want to avoid things anymore. Instead it's smarter to articulate my fears and anxieties about what I've been avoiding - admit to myself the obstacles that are keeping me stuck in place, and try to figure out how to get around those difficulties.

Here's what I've been avoiding: 
1. [Finding summer internships](http://rj17ev-jpg.github.io/posts/summer). 
  - It's always the same story. I hate applying to things, because I feel so afraid of rejection. I always put it off as long as possible: and in some cases, I didn't do it at all. The concept of applying to things and hoping for acceptance makes me feel that I'm inferior to everyone else, I'm so flawed, I don't have the same accomplishments as other people, et cetera, et cetera, so to not feel all these negative emotions it's so much easier to avoid the project entirely. 
  - Yes, I understand this is silly. Of course I'm perfectly qualified to do research: I'm a master's student, for God's sake! And I'm already doing an internship and doing reasonably well ! I have a strong interest in psychology and cognitive neuroscience, seeing that I am in a *master's program*. I have several computational skills. I've taken so many interesting classes. There's nothing stopping me from finding a fun, interesting research internship for the summer. 
2. Requesting my transcript from the administrator. 
  -  There's the overwhelming fear that I'll have a bad rank in the class, or in my track. I won't be able to take it. 
  - I'm not sure how to get around this, except to say that my grades in my classes last semester were in fact **very decent** (especially in the context of the European system) and expecting perfection is always really rather self-defeating. I was pretty happy with the grades; I will have to be okay with whatever rank I'm at. That's something I just have to find out and accept ! And if I want to make it better, I can work hard this semester.
3. Cleaning out the old food bottles and jars I emptied from the fridge.
  - Because I don't know what to do with them. They don't go directly into the trash. I have to empty them somewhere - but where ? They're not liquid and not solid. Somewhere in between. What the hell am I supposed to do ? 
4. Going running regularly. 
  - Because: the last time I ran, two days ago, I had to stop because my foot hurt very much - not because I got tired. So not running may be the smart strategy here, or maybe I just need to warm up and cool down better. I'm not going to 'run away' from this anymore - going running regularly makes my life 10000 times better, so I'll do it, starting 25th march, if I physically can. 
5. Publishing something I've written online, where people might read it. 
 - Yes, how original. I'm only the billionth writer on this planet to have this fear of being seen and judged and criticized. But, unlike all the other people who got over it and eventually perhaps even found that people *liked* their writing, I am sitting here dragging it out indefinitely. 
 -  Of course it's going to be bad ! It's my first try ! I know all this. I am going to publish my writing, knowing perfectly well it'll have a 98% chance of being absolutely terrible, and I will do it anyway, because I need to *get started* if I want to improve at it. 
6. Returning the completely wrong battery thing I got from Amazon and getting a *refund* ! 
  - Why ? because it's inconvenient. because I don't know where *exactly* I need to drop it off. because the package is heavy. 
7. Getting physical books I want to read from the ALIP. 
  - Again, because it's *inconvenient*, because it takes time and effort. The commute is long. I'll have to think about and decide which books to get, out of the infinite mountain of books on my TBR list. 
  - This is what is commonly referred to as **friction**. 
  - And this procrastination hurts me every single day, because there are many points of time during any given day when I just want to *read a book*. offline. and I have no book ! 
8. meeting new people, especially men. 
  - I can't even explain how horrible it feels to be in public anymore. because i have this desire that arises within me, on seeing certain strangers, that I want to talk to them, they seem interesting, I'd have a fun time knowing them - and I have this avoidant side of me that actively tamps that desire down. It says: hide, run away, make yourself small, make yourself invisible, you're not good enough, look at your phone. and it always feels like I'm betraying myself. beyond that - like I'm betraying a lovely, essential thing about being human - connections and friendship and solidarity. this is the worst. 
 - I'd like to somehow kill the part of me that's so so so so negative. Because the truth is that the worst possible outcome of this is in fact not even that bad - why would I care about how a stranger acts toward me ? and the best possible outcome is *beyond* amazing - worth every risk. I realized this truth long ago, already - spending time with people you like and love is one of the greatest sources of happiness in this life. so I don't want to waste all these opportunities.
