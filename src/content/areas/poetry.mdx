## What draws me to this

I really love poetry. As an English literature major I read quite a few beautiful, funny, moving poems and analyzed them to figure out what they were trying to do and how. The way I realized I still love poetry has been quite surprising, to be honest, because it hasn't been an intentional hobby of mine for a really long time. I'd fallen into this routine where if I came across a poem on Pinterest and thought it was incredibly moving, I saved it (and then never looked at it again).

However !! A magical beautiful new thing brought poetry back into focus for me !! Starting in January, my friend and I started going to 'spoken word poetry' (in english !) at the bar Au Chat Noir in Paris. I have to confess that the first time I went, I was expecting it to be rather stupid and boring. Poetry, for me, until that point, had existed only on the page. There were many things I could immediately list off that seemed sketchy about spoken word poetry: 
a) doesn't it seem too much like *slam* and that's a different game entirely 
b) we can't *go back* and reread and reflect and sit with the words, the way we do with written poems, we can't take our time and examine our first and second and third impressions of a poem. 
c) worst of all (and this is an ode to how much I've come to love it) we cannot immediately save it somewhere to come back to later. 

yes, perhaps, something in the vein of complexity is lost here - but it's more than made up for by the *people* reading their work. I can't even describe how wonderful it has been to hear some of these poets. 

## Current thread

After reading a particularly amazing poem I felt quite a strong need to understand *how* it had created that effect on me. It amazes me still how poems manage to compress so much emotion and meaning into such a compact format. I couldn't live with the mystery anymore. Clearly the way I'd been taught to analyze poetry was so elementary it was practically useless. There are far more intricate things at play, which I found out when I bothered to look at some books that would enlighten me on this: 

- _Poetic Meter and Poetic Form_ -- Paul Fussell - (meter, stanza patterns, sound) - how they shape meaning; form 
- _The Triggering Town: Lectures and Essays on Poetry and Writing_ -- Richard Hugo
    - certain images & lines - emotional force
    - poets tap into that through personal honesty and **associative thought** !! 
    - why poems matter as well as how they work
- _The Sounds of Poetry: A Brief Guide_ -- Robert Pinsky
    - lineation, rhythm, vowel and consonant play - aliveness 
- _How to Read a Poem: And Fall in Love with Poetry_ -- Edward Hirsch
    - feeling + understanding
    - **noticing** images, form, voice, music 
- _The Art of Syntax: Rhythm of Thought, Rhythm of Song_ -- Ellen Bryant Voigt
    - line breaks, sentence shape
- _The Poetry Home Repair Manual: Practical Advice for Beginning Poets_ -- Ted Kooser
- _The Music of What Happens: Poems, Poets, Critics_ -- Dana Gioia
- _The Making of a Poem: A Norton Anthology of Poetic Forms_ -- Mark Strand & E. A. … (co-editor)
    - goes into different **forms** of poetry
    - sonnets, villanelles, ghazals
