I'm in love with the *New York Times* crossword. I do it every single day. I have been doing it, on and off, for *years* now. 

I have completed some really incredible, inventive, FUN puzzles here that absolutely blew me away with their creativity, their sense of humor and their completely awesome, original way of looking at the English language. Every time I think there's nothing else in the name of puns or wordplay under the sun anyone can possibly come up with, these brilliant constructors prove me wrong. 

So, of course - I want to be like them ! I want to create a crossword puzzle. A *good* one, even. But: to get started, I'll be okay with creating the worst crossword puzzle in the history of ever, too, just so long as it exists. 

I spent a lot of time today [2026-03-22] reading this [series of articles](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/09/14/crosswords/how-to-make-a-crossword-puzzle-the-series.html) by NYT constructors on how to create a crossword puzzle. The hardest parts, to me, seem to be coming up with the theme and (especially) designing the grid. Writing the clues seems like the most fun part of the process !

Some places to start:

1. Do more puzzles - make notes about them, what works and what doesn't - especially favorite clues and answers.
2. Start your own word list - highest priority are words and phrases and names that have never been featured before !
3. Read the advice articles on [cruciverb.com](https://www.cruciverb.com/). 
4. Watch the documentary *Wordplay* that has Will Shortz in it !
